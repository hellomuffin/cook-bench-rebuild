Plan vs follow

Can it plan, or only follow a plan?

The controller results don't say why the VLM fails — bad plan, or bad execution? Two experiments on the same 9 tasks separate the two abilities (gemini-3.1-pro, 3 reps each).

① Open-loop planning

Show the model only the initial frame + recipe; it outputs the entire command list. We run it blind (no feedback) and check delivery. Tests pure planning.

② Timing (follow a correct plan)

Hand the model the verified ground-truth plan; each second it only decides advance vs wait — issue the next command when the cook is ready. Tests execution / timing. Pixels-only: the agent is shown neither the cook's position nor any coordinates in the plan, so it must read "step done?" from the video alone.

It's a better follower than planner. Given a correct plan it succeeds 81% of the time; left to plan for itself, only 59%. So a large share of controller failures are planning failures, not execution. The gap that remains under "follow" is a timing/perception problem (below).

Cells like the medium-map Caprese and the harder Pizzas show the signature pattern: 0–1/3 when planning, but 3/3 when following — it knows the steps once told, it just can't compose them itself.

Break: put down — can't put that down here — face an empty board/counter or an open pot/pan/oven. The model tries to pick up the cheese and put it down on the plate; it doesn't know raw cheese is added by holding a plate and scooping from the source. A mechanics error, not perception.

Planning errors are mechanics/order errors — across the open-loop failures the breaks are scoop / put down issued where they're illegal: confusing "scoop onto a held plate" with "pick up & put down", or assembling in the wrong order/station. The model knows the ingredients but not the exact interaction grammar.

Why following sometimes still fails — perceiving completion

Measurement honesty: an earlier version of this test leaked the answer — it showed the cook's live (x,y) position and baked (x,y) coordinates into the plan, so the model could confirm "arrived" by coordinate-matching instead of looking. That inflated FOLLOW to 88%. With both removed (pixels-only), it is 81% (22/27) — and the failures all share one cause.

Remaining following-failures:

  • Pizza (hard-nops·easy-map): issued 18/25, advanced too early 59× — kept ordering the next step before the cook finished, so steps were dropped and it ran out of time.
  • Pizza (hard-nops·easy-map): issued 18/25, advanced too early 68× — kept ordering the next step before the cook finished, so steps were dropped and it ran out of time.

The residual failure is perceiving step-completion from pixels. The clearest case — hard_nops_medium_map, which passed 3/3 only with the coordinate crutch and went 0/3 pixels-only: the cook is already holding the dough (the "pick up" succeeded), yet the model repeats "the cook has not picked up the dough yet" 98 times and never advances — it cannot see the held item in the frame. Same root cause across the board: it can't tell from pixels that an action finished.

This is exactly what the new controller feedback channel targets: a command received and executed line tells the model the pickup worked without it having to perceive the held item; agent++/+++ additionally surface position and holding […].
