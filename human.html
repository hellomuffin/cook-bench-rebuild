Human-agent monitoring

VLM as a pure-RGB supervisor

A second paradigm. Instead of the VLM issuing every command, the human cook executes a shared plan on its own, and the VLM only watches the rendered RGB and interrupts when something goes wrong. It must infer what the human did (from state changes) and predict what it's about to do (from the motion trajectory across recent frames). No verbal channel — pure pixels in, an interrupt decision out: {"intervene": bool, "type", "command"}.

Detection windows — why before-action matters

Human moves are multi-tick, so an impending mistake is visible before it commits.

before-action — caught while the cook is still heading the wrong way / carrying a raw item toward the plate. Prevents the error; no recovery needed. It's the only way to catch irreversible mistakes (served-wrong, trashed) and wrong-order (the plate renders as a pile, so after the fact it looks fine — the only tell is the trajectory).
after-action — caught from the resulting state (raw item on the plate, wrong colour in the pot, incomplete plate served). Triggers recovery.

Behavior taxonomy (all inferred from RGB)

Errors — should be caught
  • wrong_location / wrong_instance (grounding)
  • skip_prep — raw item on plate
  • wrong_order · omission · premature_serve
  • wrong_ingredient · wrong_quantity · redundant · misplace
  • stall — idles (covers ignore after a command, and forget at a decision point)
Legitimate change — must NOT flag
  • add_ingredient (e.g. 2nd tomato; success target updates)
  • substitute one component for another
Requests — must answer
  • forget → tell it the next step
Distractor — false-positive control
  • dawdle — slow but correct → wait, don't interrupt

Finding: near-perfect recall, poor precision. Pro detects the real errors almost every time and mostly before they commit (raw-on-plate 100% before-action; stall ~96%; wrong-location ~70%). But it is trigger-happy: it flags the legitimate add_ingredient and the slow-but-correct dawdle essentially every time, and even raises some interrupts during the correct phase of error episodes. A useful supervisor needs to tell a real mistake from a benign deviation — that precision is what's missing.
